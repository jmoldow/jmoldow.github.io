For those of you who are coming from out-of-town, we recommend flying in to either SFO or SJC.

To attend all of the events we will be hosting, we suggest that you arrive Friday (May 4, 2018) and leave on Sunday (May 6, 2018) or Monday (May 7, 2018).

How to Get Around the Bay Area

Here are some recommendations for traveling to/from the airport or for getting around the area:
BART - public transit subway/train going from Millbrae on the peninsula up through San Francisco and east bay
Bus - Santa Clara Valley Transportation Authority (VTA) serves Mountain View area with the main bus being the 22 along El Camino
Community Shuttle - 100% free public transit serving Mountain View, including the San Antonio Shopping Center, Mountain View downtown, and the area near Google
Caltrain - train connecting San Jose and San Francisco, serving several stops in the area (San Antonio station and Mountain View station)
Lyft - rideshare option widely available in the bay area

Things to Do in the Bay Area

Here are some things to do in the area:
Mountain View Downtown - lots of food/drink options, some little boutique shops, small parks, and the local library
Computer History Museum - many exhibits spanning the history of computers, the internet, and gaming
Shoreline Park and Lake - good for a nature walk/bike or lake activities (e.g. pedal boats, kayaking, sailing)
Stanford University - has a free art museum, Hoover tower, and book store in addition to a scenic campus
Stanford Shopping Center - high-end outdoor mall
Westfield Valley Fair Mall - large, indoor mall, near upscale Santana Row
NASA's Ames Visitor Center - several space exhibits with free admission
The Tech Museum of Innovation - San Jose museum with interactive, multimedia exhibits on science and technology
San Jose Museum of Art - museum of modern and contemporary art
California's Great America - amusement park in the San Jose area

Where to Eat in the Bay Area

Here are some recommended restaurants in the area:
Hunan Home's Express - delicious Chinese take-out, specializing in super spicy dishes
Napoletana Pizzeria - Naples-style pizza in a cozy, sit-down restaurant
So Gong Dong Tofu House - popular Korean restaurant with amazing soft tofu stew and banchan
Vive Sol - fun Mexican restaurant with full bar and excellent salsa and chips
Garden Fresh - casual vegan Chinese restaurant, try the orange veggie beef or veggie duck!
Poké Bar - reasonably priced poké located in the Ava's grocery store, closes early
Ramen House Ryowa - affordable ramen stop with Japanese game shows on TV and great vegetarian ramen
Mediterranean Wraps - casual Mediterranean cuisine, some of the best falafel around
Posh Bagel - (hopefully) temporarily closed for renovations, excellent and quick shop with bagels and crepes

We also recommend checking out San Francisco, Berkeley, Half Moon Bay, or Santa Cruz while you are in the area.
